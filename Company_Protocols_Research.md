# Company Protocols: Virtual & Collaborative Environments

## Research Summary
The following protocols are based on 2024-2025 industry standards, including the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0**, **Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)**, and modern **Remote-First Communication** frameworks.

---

## Protocol 1: Cyber Safety for Virtual Environments
**Objective:** To establish a "Zero Trust" security posture that protects company assets, data, and identities in virtual and remote-work settings.

### 1. Identity & Access Management
*   **Zero Trust Posture:** "Never trust, always verify." Every access request is authenticated and authorized based on all available data points (user identity, device health, location).
*   **Phishing-Resistant MFA:** Mandatory use of FIDO2 hardware keys or biometrics. SMS and standard TOTP (app-based codes) are phased out for high-privilege access.
*   **Least Privilege:** Access is restricted to the minimum level required for a role (RBAC) and provided on a "Just-in-Time" basis where possible.

### 2. Infrastructure & Device Security
*   **Endpoint Protection:** Continuous monitoring via Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) on all devices.
*   **Virtual Network Micro-segmentation:** Isolation of workloads to prevent lateral movement of threats.
*   **Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM):** Proactive identification of vulnerabilities in virtual machine (VM) configurations and cloud workloads.

### 3. Data & AI Governance
*   **Secure Collaboration:** Mandatory end-to-end encryption for all virtual communication platforms.
*   **AI Usage Policy:** Prohibits the input of sensitive company data into public Generative AI models.

## Protocol 2: Communication & Collaboration in Virtual Environments
**Objective:** To ensure high-clarity, inclusive, and efficient collaboration across distributed teams while minimizing "digital fatigue."

### 1. Communication Standards
*   **Async-First Mindset:** Documentation and asynchronous messaging (Slack, Notion, Jira) are the primary modes of communication to respect deep-work time.
*   **Clarity & Context:** Written messages must include sufficient context and clear "Action Required" or "For Info Only" tags.

### 2. Virtual Meeting Excellence
*   **Structured Engagement:** Mandatory agendas and "Action Item" summaries for every call.
*   **The 5-Minute Human Rule:** Start meetings with brief personal check-ins to build rapport and psychological safety.
*   **Inclusive Facilitation:** Use of digital whiteboards (Miro) and polls to ensure all team members can contribute regardless of their verbal communication style.

### 3. Cultural & Operational Boundaries
*   **Core Collaboration Hours:** Defined windows for synchronous availability across time zones.
*   **Transparency:** Decisions made in private chats or calls must be summarized in public project channels to maintain a "Single Source of Truth."
