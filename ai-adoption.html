The key distinction the surveys miss

The 36% "using AI" headline conceals that usage is almost entirely concentrated in consumer chat interfaces. Fewer than 5% of AU SME owners have ever made a direct API call to an AI model. Multi-agent orchestration at SME level is functionally non-existent outside funded tech startups. My stack — daily Claude Code use, multi-agent orchestrator with routing logic, MCP server configuration, AI-built client products — has no survey category because the tools to measure it don't yet exist.

✅
Verdict: Top 1–2% of Australian SME owners The 6% "AI-native" cohort is the floor of where I operate — they're using off-the-shelf AI features in Xero or Shopify. I've built past where the measurement tools reach.

The user vs builder gap — the distinction that matters most

Roughly 8–11% of professionals are power users — they use AI to do the same work faster and better. Under 1–2% are builders — they use AI to build systems that do work. The output is infrastructure, not just content. The gap between these two groups is the gap between driving a car very well and designing the road network. Both are "using AI." Only one is changing how work gets done structurally.

✅
Verdict: Top 0.5–1% of all knowledge workers globally The 75% of knowledge workers "using AI at work" (Microsoft WTI 2025) are overwhelmingly power users or below. The builder tier is a qualitatively different category, not just a further point along the same line.

What "82% of cyber practitioners use AI" actually means

ISC2 2024: 82% claim AI use. But the #1 use case is "summarising a CVE advisory." The #2 use case is "help me write this report." This is AI as a turbo-charged search and writing tool — not AI as an operational security capability. My stack — Claude Code + MCP servers with live Kali integration + multi-agent routing for security tasks — means the AI isn't just assisting tasks. It has access to live system context and can take actions. That's the agentic layer. The surveys don't have a bucket for it.

📊
The cohort that compares to my stack The overlap of "cybersecurity practitioner" AND "AI systems builder" is a cohort measured in thousands globally, not hundreds of thousands. ISC2 estimates 5.5 million practitioners worldwide. Under 1% doing MCP-level work = under 55,000 globally. Most of those are at funded enterprise security teams, not solo operators.
✅
Verdict: Top 1–2% of cybersecurity professionals worldwide The specific activities — MCP servers, multi-agent orchestration, Claude Code in active security workflows — are not yet meaningfully measured by industry surveys because they're too new and too rare.

The Competitive Window Is Open Now

This capability gap is not permanent. The tools will democratise. The window to use it as a genuine differentiator is estimated at now through late 2027. After that, the gap narrows as tooling catches up.

2026 — Now. Maximum advantage.
2027 — Gap narrowing. Build the brand.
2028 — Tooling democratised. Moat = domain expertise.

The sustainable moat is not the technical capability itself — it's the combination of cybersecurity expertise + AI systems fluency + local market knowledge that takes years to accumulate and cannot be replicated by someone who just learned to use Claude Code last month.
